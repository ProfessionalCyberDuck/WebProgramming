What Is Internet Addiction?

Internet addiction, or sometimes referred to as Internet Addiction Disorder (IAD), is a pattern of excessive or poorly managed online behavior that interferes with daily life. It goes beyond just “using the internet a lot.” Rather, it includes emotional dependence on digital activities, difficulty regulating time spent online, and detrimental effects on relationships, employment, or mental health.

Common Types of Internet Addiction

  • Social Media Addiction – Constant scrolling, checking notifications, or posting
  • Online Gaming Addiction – Excessive time spent on gaming
  • Streaming or Video Addiction – Watching videos for hours without stopping
  • Online Gambling – Compulsive betting behavior
  • Online Shopping Addiction – Compulsive browsing or buying

Anybody can become addicted to the internet regardless of age or background. It usually takes time to develop and can go undetected until it starts to interfere with daily activities.
